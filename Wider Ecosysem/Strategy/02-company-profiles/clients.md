## The Validation-First Methodology

### Why Small Before Large

Every project carries risk. The traditional approach is to estimate, plan, budget, commit—and discover problems after significant investment. The validation-first approach reverses this.

**How it works:**

1. **Identify the critical assumption**
   What needs to be true for this project to succeed? What's the riskiest part?

2. **Design a small experiment**
   Build the smallest thing that tests the assumption. Not a prototype—a probe.

3. **Execute and measure**
   Deliver the experiment. Collect data. Observe what happens.

4. **Decision gate**
   Based on evidence, decide: proceed, pivot, or stop. Not based on hope—based on information.

5. **Scale what works**
   When the experiment succeeds, expand with confidence. The risk has been reduced.

**What this means for you:**
You don't commit large budgets to uncertain outcomes. You commit small budgets to learning, then large budgets to proven approaches. The methodology protects your investment by ensuring it flows to what works.
